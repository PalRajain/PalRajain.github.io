Student's Attitude: A Reflection of Teachers' Commitment

Rupa Rathee, Pallavi Rajain

Abstract

The effective attitudes and actions employed by teachers ultimately can make a positive difference in the lives of their students. The present study has been conducted to know the students’ attitude towards teachers in Management Institutes and to explore whether undergraduate and post-graduate students have similar attitude towards the commitment of the management teachers. Descriptive research design has been used in the study. The primary data has been collected from the students of management programs in National Capital Region with the help of a structured questionnaire. Assessment of the commitment of the management teachers by the students has been obtained with the help of the statements on a five-point scale. The sample size of the study is 104. The sample had been drawn using convenience sampling. Out of 104 respondents 48 respondents were undergraduates while 56 respondents were postgraduates. The analysis has been done with the help of SPSS version 20.0 and using descriptive statistics like means and standard deviation. Independent samples t-test has also been applied. The study reveals that the majority of the students have a positive attitude towards most of the management teachers and they believe that the teachers are committed to their jobs.
